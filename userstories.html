HealthCare

Stories about potential users of the final app

Meet Peter

Peter lives in nigeria and has been working with the nigerian civil defence for the passed 30 years retiring last year.Peter was diagnosed with a diabetic condition two years ago and has been on medications since then.Peter will like to control his calorie intake, monitor his blood sugar level and his blood pressure so as to live a long healthy life.

His Goals

  • To eat less unhealthy food
  • To monitor his sugar and blood pressure levels
  • To live a heathy life
  • To regularly take his medications

Frustrations

  • Aware to his high calorie intake which he dose not monitor
  • His need for a suitable platform by which he can monitor and record his blood sugar and blood presure
  • Problem of not remembering to take his medication as at when due

Task Analysis

The following use cases have been identified and are described as tasks

  • Configuring the healthcare application for a medication reminder
  • Monitor blood sugar level over a period of four weeks
  • Monitor blood pressure level over a period of four weeks
  • Monitor calorie intake for a period of four weeks
  • Configuring the healthcare application for setting goals

Task 1 - Configuring the healthcare application for medication reminder

  1. Peter installs the app from Google playstore
  2. He launches the application and selects the sign-up option
  3. He completes the sign-up form by inputting his email and password
  4. He selects login and inputs his email and password to login
  5. He is presented with the home screen page where he can chose between filling in blood pressure,blood sugar,medication,goals and calories
  6. He selects the medication reminder button
  7. He is presented with the medication reminder dashboard which should a list of all the medications Peter will want to be reminded off
  8. He selects the plus icon at the bottom of the page and he is presented with a form to add the necessary details about the drug such as name.start date,end date, units,instruction and repeats
  9. He click on the submit and he is taken back to the drug dash board page to see that the drug is added to it.
  10. He is constantly reminded to take his medication as at when due.

Task 2 - Monitor blood sugar level over a period of four weeks

  1. Peter is concerned with his blood sugar level and wants to monitor it on a daily bases for a period of time
  2. He logs into the application
  3. He is presented with the home screen page where he can chose between filling in blood pressure,blood sugar,medication,goals and calories
  4. He selects the blood sugar button
  5. He is taken to the blood sugar dashboard page where all blood sugar readings entered by peter will be recorded.
  6. He clicks on the plus icon to add a blood sugar readings and he is presented with a page to add blood sugar
  7. He enters the needed details such as sugar concentration,date,time,the period measures and clicks on submit
  8. He is taken back to the blood sugar dashboard where he sees the reading he just entered together with a graph plotted with the readings against the normal blood sugar level

Task 3 - Monitor blood sugar pressure over a period of four weeks

  1. Peter is concerned about the current rise of his blood pressure and wants to keep track of this over a period of time
  2. He logs into the application
  3. He is presented with the home screen page where he can chose between filling in blood pressure,blood sugar,medication,goals and calories
  4. He selects the blood pressure button
  5. He is taken to the blood pressure dashboard page where all blood pressure readings entered by Peter will be recorded.
  6. He clicks on the plus icon to add a blood pressure readings and he is presented with a page to add blood pressure
  7. He enters the needed details such as systolic pressure,diastolic pressure,measured arm date, time and clicks on submit
  8. He is taken back to the blood sugar dashboard where he see the reading he just entered together with a graph plotted with the readings against the normal blood pressure level

Task 4 - Monitor calorie intake for a period of four weeks

  1. Peter is concerned about monitoring his current calorie intake and would like to keep track of it over a period of time
  2. He logs into the application
  3. He is presented with the home screen page where he can chose between filling in blood pressure,blood sugar,medication,goals and calories
  4. He selects the calories button
  5. He is taken to the calorie dashboard page where all calorie readings entered by Peter will be recorded
  6. He clicks on the plus icon to add a calorie readings by adding the associated food
  7. He is presented with a page where he can add the food he has taken for a certain period and the date and time the food was eaten
  8. He further scrolls down the page to add the various contents of the food that was taken together with the quantity taken as the application automatically calculated the calories in those food content.
  9. When he is done adding the food contents he clicks on the proceed button
  10. A summary page is presented to him showing him the choices that he selected and also gives him the option to go back and make changes to those option that was selected
  11. On the confirmation page he clicks on submit and he is taken back to the calories dashboard where he see the reading he just entered together with a graph plotted with the readings against the normal calories for a human being.

Task 5 - Configuring the healthcare application for setting goals

  1. peter would like to enter a specific goals to monitor his daily calorie readings with on the application. this is because peter would like to either losse or gain weight
  2. He logs into the application
  3. He is presented with the home screen page where he can chose between filling in blood pressure,blood sugar,medication,goals and calories
  4. He selects the goal button
  5. He is taken to the goals dashboard where all calories goals entered by Peter are recorded
  6. He clicks on the calories card view and is taken to the goal page where he can alter the calories reading to any amount of his choice and submits.
  7. Finally he is taken back to the home page
